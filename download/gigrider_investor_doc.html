Revenue Deep Dive

How Each Stream Works

Every revenue stream is designed so that GigRider only earns when riders earn more — creating a self-reinforcing flywheel of growth and trust.

01 — Freemium Subscriptions
Free tier ensures maximum adoption and network effects. Pro (Rs 299/mo) unlocks Auto-Rank, Smart Stack, and countdown timers. Elite (Rs 799/mo) adds financial services, auto-accept rules, and tax filing. Fleet (Rs 1,999/mo) serves fleet operators managing 5+ riders with analytics dashboards.
Rs 27.9 Cr/month
02 — Commission per Order
The single largest revenue stream. A 2% fee on every order is negligible for riders who save time and fuel, but at massive scale generates Rs 187.5 Crore monthly. Riders earn 15-25% more even after the fee, making this a win-win proposition that aligns incentives perfectly.
Rs 187.5 Cr/month
03 — Financial Services
Delivery riders are the most underserved demographic in finance. Micro-loans (Rs 50-80K for vehicle purchase at 18-24% interest), pay-per-trip accident insurance (Rs 5-10/day), and auto-savings wallets. GigRider's real-time earnings data enables credit scoring no bank can match.
Rs 35 Cr/month
04 — Data Monetization
Aggregated, anonymized delivery intelligence sold as subscriptions: city-level analytics for restaurant chains (Rs 5-25K/mo), demand heatmaps for dark kitchens (Rs 10-50K/mo), custom research for investors (Rs 1-5 Lakh), and platform intelligence products (Rs 50K-2L/mo). 90%+ margins.
Rs 12.5 Cr/month
05 — B2B Enterprise API
The core aggregation technology is a product in itself. Restaurant chains, cloud kitchens, and new delivery platforms need a unified API to interact with multiple platforms. Per-call fees (Rs 0.50-2.00), monthly SaaS subscriptions (Rs 10K-1L/mo), and custom enterprise contracts (Rs 5-20L/year).
Rs 15 Cr/month
06 — White-Label Licensing
The rider aggregation problem exists in every country. License technology to local partners with Rs 20-50 Lakh setup fee, 15-25% revenue share, and Rs 5-15 Lakh annual maintenance. 20 international partners generate Rs 4-10 Cr upfront plus Rs 10-25 Cr annually.
Rs 8 Cr/month
07 — Advertising
The home screen is prime real estate viewed dozens of times daily. Sponsored order placement (Rs 5-20 per order), banner ads on earnings screen (Rs 50K-2L/mo per city), push notification partnerships (Rs 2-5 per click), and branded vehicle decals (Rs 500-1,500/mo per rider).
Rs 10 Cr/month
08 — Rider Marketplace
Direct restaurant-rider connection bypassing delivery platforms entirely. GigRider charges 5-8% vs. platforms' 20-30%. Creates powerful network effects: more restaurants attract more riders, more riders attract more restaurants. 10K restaurants at 20 orders/day generates Rs 4.5 Cr monthly.
Rs 22.5 Cr/month
09 — Training & Certification
Paid courses: Food Safety Certification (Rs 499), Route Optimization (Rs 299), Financial Literacy (Rs 149). Tax filing services (Rs 499-999), GST registration (Rs 1,499), and legal document services. 100K enrollments/year at Rs 350 average generates Rs 3.5 Cr annually with very high margins.
Rs 3 Cr/month
10 — Referral Partnerships
Riders spend Rs 3,000+/month on fuel and Rs 500 on mobile recharges. Partner with telecom companies, petrol pumps, service centers, and equipment brands for 3-10% commission. 200K riders using partner services generates Rs 2.1-7 Crore monthly commission revenue.
Rs 20 Cr/month

Investor Q&A + Key Metrics

Anticipated Tough Questions

Won't delivery platforms block GigRider?
GigRider is a rider productivity tool, not a competing platform. We aggregate notifications riders already receive on their own devices. Legally, this is equivalent to a notification manager. Precedent: Para operated the same model in the US and was acquired by DoorDash for USD 50M+. Platforms benefit from riders who complete more deliveries, creating alignment of interests.
How do you prevent the commission from alienating riders?
Riders earn 15-25% more using GigRider. Even after a 2% commission, net income increases by 13-23%. We demonstrate this with an "Earnings Boost" counter in the app. Riders who see Rs 500 extra daily earnings happily pay Rs 5 per order. The Free tier pays zero commission.
What about platforms building their own multi-app features?
No platform will help riders use a competitor. Swiggy will never show Zomato orders. This structural conflict of interest means multi-platform aggregation can only come from a neutral third party. GigRider's neutrality is its moat. Riders also won't trust a platform to fairly rank competitor orders.
How do you verify the financial projections?
Benchmarked against Paytm/PhonePe (commission models), BharatPe/KreditBee (gig worker lending), and Razorpay/BillDesk (per-transaction API fees). We use conservative conversion rates (12% vs. industry 15-20%), conservative commission (2% vs. potential 3-5%), and linear growth vs. exponential growth seen in ride-hailing.

Part III — Patent Strategy & Action Plan

Three Patent Applications

Three patents create three walls around your moat. Competitors cannot simply design around one.

Patent I
Multi-Platform Delivery Order Aggregation & Prioritization
Core system for intercepting notifications from multiple delivery platforms, normalizing data into a unified structure (UODS), and ranking orders using a multi-variable algorithm with 6 weighted factors: earnings per km, earnings per minute, countdown urgency, pickup proximity, platform reliability, and rider history. Includes adaptive weight adjustment through machine learning feedback loop.
Patent II
Multi-Platform Gig Credit Scoring (MPGCS)
Credit scoring system (300-900 range) using cross-platform delivery performance data: order completion rate (20%), earnings stability index (20%), on-time delivery rate (15%), working consistency index (15%), platform diversity score (10%), customer satisfaction composite (10%), and tenure factor (10%). Enables automated loan origination and insurance underwriting.
Patent III
Geographic Delivery Batching Across Independent Platforms
Smart Stack technology identifying batchable orders across platforms using Haversine proximity detection (2km pickup, 3km delivery thresholds), modified TSP route optimization with real-time traffic data, and Stack Score computation measuring efficiency gain of batched vs. individual delivery routes.
